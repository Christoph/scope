Welcome to Graphite

Graphite is a tool that helps you optimize the way you find and read news. It graphically clusters news articles by similarity, allowing you to get an instant overview over their structure and interact with news in a completely new way.

On top of that, Graphite produces a list of reading suggestions that lets you maximze the breadth of your reading. For this it follows a simple recipe: Pick that article from the largest topic cluster (i.e. the hottest topic) that has the most overlap with the other articles from that cluster, then do the same for the second largest cluster, etc. In this way, by following the suggestion list, you get the most out of your news, however much time you have to read (click here to learn more about how Graphite works.)

Graphite can work its magic on any bunch of articles that you feed it. On this page you see its ouput on a selection of about 600 recently published articles from around 40 global newspapers. Make the most of Graphite by filtering the above graph, starting your own custom search, creating a mail alert and managing your searches with a Graphite-profile.

Interacting with the news

At the heart of graphite are its graphs and the way in which they allow you to interact with the news. Links between nodes indicate the the articles are closely related. Hover over an article-node to see its title and description, click to fix the box and then click the title to browse directly to the article.

Hover over a suggestion in the list on the left side to see the cluster and related articles from the cluster. Again, clicking on the suggestion title brings you to the article, while clicking anywhere else in the suggestion bar freezes the cluster and lets you explore the cluster and related articles (to unfreeze, click the suggestion bar again).

Adjust the viewing options as you please by including or excluding unconnected articles or setting the text display.

Finally, enjoy discovering the news in a different way, for example drag clusters along or follow how topics change along chains. Let me know about your experience, insights, critcism, ideas!
